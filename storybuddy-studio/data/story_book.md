# My Amazing Adventure

## Chapter 1: The Door That Should Not Be There

The Whispering Forest smelled like rain before rain even arrived. Alex noticed it first — that sharp, electric tingle in the air that makes the back of your neck prickle. The ancient trees hummed louder than usual, their low song vibrating through the mossy ground and right up through Alex's trainers. Something was different tonight. Something was *waiting*.

Then ARWA came sprinting through the tree line, her school bag bouncing wildly, her hair soaked from the sudden downpour that had chased her all the way from the school gates. "I was just walking home," she gasped, hands on her knees, "and the rain — it just *started*. Out of nowhere. Like the sky was trying to tell me something!" She looked up, eyes wide. And that's when all three of them saw it.

Behind the oldest tree in the forest — the one so wide it would take five children holding hands to hug it — a door stood impossibly in the air. It glowed soft green, like a firefly trapped in glass. Strange symbols swirled across its surface, and from underneath the crack at the bottom, golden light poured out like warm honey. Pip the fox took one slow step forward, nose twitching.

"A door in the woods, glowing and bright — whatever is in there, it fills me with delight!" Pip whispered, ears flat, tail curled tight with excitement.

Alex reached out and touched the symbols. They were warm. They *pulsed*. And somewhere deep inside that golden light, something knocked back.
